ABOUT ME

Who am I?

My name is Jack Sturzaker, I'm 26 years old and based in Manchester. I am currently learning foundational front-end as a means of starting a career in UX. I don't come from a tech or even STEM background having grauduated with a degree in History in 2017. Since then, I've done a variety of jobs from teaching English in Spain to waiting tables!

Outside of learning to code, I enjoy cooking (or maybe just eating?) and can be found compulsively checking my fantasy football team every Saturday!

Why am I learning to code?

I've always been interested by how the way things are designed affect how people interact with them, from public spaces to kitchen appliances.

Growing up in the noughties, I have experienced my fair share of cluttered pages with small text that were almost innavigable. What appeals to me about front-end development and UX is that it gives you an opportunity to creatively bridge problems of accessibility through thoughtful and aesthetically pleasing design.

How am I learning to code?

My first experience of coding was spent banging my head against a wall trying to learn JavaScript in the back-end on Code Academy about a year ago, probably not the best idea as somebody with little experience in logic!

Since then, I have had much more luck learning front-end technologies through an 8-week course run by MadLab , a grassroots tech charity based in Manchester. I have also supplemented this with some Superhi courses, an online creative and technology education startup. Combined, these have given me a great foundation in HTML and CSS, as well as a little bit of JavaScript!
